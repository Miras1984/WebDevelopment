Cottage cheese pancakes

  • Time: 25 min
  • Serves: 5 people
Ingredients Quantity
Cottage cheese
Egg
Flour
Oil 		500g
2
300g
50ml

Step 1: Put all the cottage cheese in a saucepan and mash it with a fork so that there are no large lumps left in it. Break eggs into it, add sugar and mix everything thoroughly. It is better not to use too dry or too wet cottage cheese, otherwise the cheesecakes will fall apart during the cooking process.
Step 2: Pour 5 tablespoons (with a slide) of flour into the cottage cheese and mix thoroughly. You can add a little more flour, then the cheesecakes will turn out more dense. Or you can add a little less flour, and then the cheesecakes will be more tender. As a result, you should get a homogeneous mass, from which it will be possible to sculpt cheesecakes.
Step 3: Place a frying pan over medium heat and pour sunflower oil into it.

Brownie

  • Time: 40 min
  • Serves: 2 people
Ingredients Quantity
Dark chocolate
Butter
Brown sugar
Egg
Flour
Walnut 		100g
180g
200g
4
100g
100g

Step 1:Break the chocolate into pieces and melt together with the butter in a water bath, stirring all the time with a spatula or a wooden spoon. Remove the resulting thick chocolate sauce from the water bath and leave to cool.
Step 2: In the meantime, mix the eggs with one hundred grams of brown sugar: break the eggs into a separate bowl and beat, gradually adding sugar. You can beat with a mixer or by hand - as you like - but not less than two and a half to three minutes.
Step 3: Chop the walnuts with a sharp knife on a cutting board. You can pre-fry them in a dry frying pan until the aroma appears, but this is an optional option.
Step 4:You can serve brownies just like that, or you can sprinkle powdered sugar on top, or arrange the squares on plates and decorate each serving with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.
